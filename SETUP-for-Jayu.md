# Getting Lumi onto your phone

A quick, one-time setup. After this, Lumi is just an app on your phone.

## 1. Get the app file (APK)
Bjarke will push this project to GitHub. When he does, GitHub builds the app
automatically:
1. Open the repo on GitHub → **Actions** tab.
2. Click the latest **Build APK** run (green check).
3. Under **Artifacts**, download **lumi-debug** and unzip it to get
   `app-debug.apk`.
4. Send that file to your phone (email, Drive, or cable).

## 2. Install it
1. Tap the `app-debug.apk` on your phone.
2. Android may ask to allow installing from this source — say yes.
3. Open **Lumi**.

## 3. First use
- Tap **Take today's photo**. A camera opens with an oval guide — just line
  your face up inside the oval and tap the round shutter button. Framing your
  face the same way each day gives the best before/after.
- Allow the camera, and (optionally) notifications so the daily reminder works.
- Set a reminder time in **Settings** if you like.

## Using it day to day
- **Today**: photo + how your skin looks + a note.
- **Routine**: tick the products you used and log your LED mask session.
- **Progress**: slide between your first and latest photo, see your trend.
- **Skin Check**: a quick non-medical read of common skin patterns.
- **Ask an AI**: shares a photo to Claude, Gemini, or ChatGPT for a friendly,
  non-medical read. Your photos stay on your phone until you choose to share.

## A note on safety
Lumi helps you track and care for your skin — it can't diagnose. The light masks
it supports are **LED**, never UV. For anything sore, spreading, or a mole that's
changing, please see a dermatologist. 💛
